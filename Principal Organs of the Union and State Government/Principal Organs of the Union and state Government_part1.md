## Q.1) The role of the President of India becomes more significant during a minority government and a coalition government. Explain. (2021) (15)

### üéØ Core Theme
The President's role, typically ceremonial in the Westminster model, transforms into pivotal constitutional guardianship during minority/coalition governments‚Äîexercising crucial discretionary powers in government formation, ensuring democratic propriety through political consultations, and maintaining constitutional integrity during periods of political instability.

### üîë Key Recall Terms
President's Role | Westminster Model | Minority Government | Coalition Government | Discretionary Powers | Government Formation | Trust Vote | Granville Austin | Guardian Role | K.R. Narayanan Precedent | Legislative Assent | Return Bills | Political Instability

### üìù Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- President's role largely ceremonial under Indian Constitution
- Based on Westminster model with symbolic duties
- Significance escalates during political instability
- Minority government: No single party majority in Lok Sabha
- Coalition government: Multi-party alliances common since 1980s
- Role transcends ceremonial functions to become pivotal

**Body:**

*President's Discretion in Government Formation:*
- Crucial during minority governments
- No single party has outright Lok Sabha majority
- President determines which party has "best chance" of commanding majority
- Involves intricate political judgments and consultations
- More than figurehead role
- Granville Austin: President plays "guardian" role
- Ensures democratic governance and stability

*Coalition Government Context:*
- Increasingly common since 1980s
- Formed through alliances between multiple parties
- Face challenges of cohesion and stability
- President ensures formation process follows constitutional propriety
- May facilitate dialogue between parties
- Can decide when to call for trust vote
- Tests government's majority in Parliament

*Legislative Process Role:*
- Enhanced during coalition/minority governments
- Assent to legislation typically formality
- Can use discretionary powers to return bills for reconsideration
- Exception: Cannot return money bills
- Actions rare but underscore potential influence
- Particularly important in politically sensitive contexts

*Historical Precedents:*
- Powers not merely theoretical
- Late 1990s political turmoil example
- President K.R. Narayanan set precedents
- Insisted on letters of support from allies before inviting leaders
- Ensured transparency in government formation
- Adherence to democratic principles

**Conclusion:**
President's role significantly escalates during minority/coalition governments; discretionary powers instrumental in safeguarding constitutional integrity and democratic governance; moments of political flux test resilience of democratic institutions and highlight President's capacity to influence national politics beyond ceremonial duties.

## Q.2) Has the thrust of government tilted towards executive in contemporary Indian Politics? Give your arguments. (2020) (15)

### üéØ Core Theme

### üìù Answer Structure

- Discernible shift in power balance towards executive

- Reflected in centralization of decision-making

- Enhanced executive role and influence in governance

**Body:**

*PMO Centralization:*
- Pratap Bhanu Mehta: Concentration of power in executive
- Particularly within PMO
- Significant increase in last few decades
- Justified on grounds of efficiency
- Need for strong leadership in governance
- Critics: May undermine collective decision-making
- Threatens accountability fundamental to parliamentary democracy

*Ordinances as Legislative Tool:*
- Point of contention
- Constitution allows ordinances with same force as laws
- Issued when Parliament not in session

- Frequent use raises concerns
- Bypasses legislative scrutiny of Parliament
- PRS Legislative Research: Noticeable increase in recent years

*Judicial-Executive Interactions:*
- Judiciary as check on executive power
- Executive actions subject to judicial scrutiny
- Supreme Court judgments: Right to Privacy, Section 377 decriminalization
- Demonstrates judiciary's critical role
- Safeguarding constitutional values
- Sometimes in opposition to executive actions

*Executive Agencies and Digital Governance:*
- Increasing role of executive agencies
- Use of digital governance tools
- Augmented executive's reach and efficiency
- Enhances policy implementation and service delivery
- Necessitates robust oversight mechanisms
- Accountability to prevent potential overreach

**Conclusion:**
Evidence of tilt towards executive is nuanced and multifaceted; involves complex interactions among government branches; concentration of power, ordinance use, and centralized decision-making suggest enhanced executive role; yet robustness of democratic institutions including judiciary and legislative oversight maintain balance of power, highlighting dynamic nature of India's constitutional democracy.

## Q.4) Examine the evolution of the jurisdiction of the Supreme Court of India as a Constitutional Court. (2021) (15)

### üéØ Core Theme
The Supreme Court's jurisdiction has evolved from apex dispute-resolver to dynamic Constitutional guardian‚Äîexpanding through Golaknath's judicial review assertion, Kesavananda Bharati's basic structure doctrine, PIL revolution for marginalized access, and progressive rights interpretation like privacy, adapting to democracy's changing needs.

### üîë Key Recall Terms
Supreme Court | Constitutional Court | Article 131 | Original Jurisdiction | Golaknath 1967 | Kesavananda Bharati 1973 | Basic Structure | Public Interest Litigation | PIL | Hussainara Khatoon | Locus Standi | Pratap Bhanu Mehta | Puttaswamy | Right to Privacy | Article 21 | Judicial Activism

### üìù Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- Supreme Court established in 1950
- Significant evolution in jurisdiction and role
- Response to changing needs of Indian society
- Governance complexities in world's largest democracy
- Initially: Apex body for disputes and constitutional interpretation
- Jurisdiction expanded over years
- Pivotal institution in democratic framework

**Body:**

*Original Jurisdiction:*
- Defined in Article 131
- Hears disputes between Union and states
- Or among states themselves directly
- Underscores role in maintaining federal structure
- Greater expansion in appellate and writ jurisdiction

*Golaknath Landmark (1967):*
- Golaknath v. State of Punjab
- Declared Fundamental Rights cannot be amended by Parliament
- Assertion of judicial review
- Set stage for active role in safeguarding citizens' rights

*Kesavananda Bharati Revolution (1973):*
- Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala
- Underscored Court's constitutional guardianship
- Established basic structure doctrine
- Parliament has wide amendment powers
- BUT cannot alter Constitution's fundamental framework
- Cornerstone of reviewing constitutional amendments

*PIL Revolution:*
- Advent in late 20th century
- Hussainara Khatoon v. State of Bihar notable case
- Pivotal shift towards accessible justice
- For India's poor and marginalized
- Relaxed traditional locus standi requirement
- Enabled individuals/organizations to file on behalf of others
- Significantly broadened role in public grievances
- Ensuring social justice

*Judicial Activism Era:*
- Wide range of issues intervention
- Environmental protection to corruption
- Education and health rights
- Stepping into executive and legislative domains
- Not without criticism
- Pratap Bhanu Mehta: Caution against judicial overreach
- Potential overreach into policy domains

*Recent Expansions:*
- Right to Privacy interpretation
- Justice K.S. Puttaswamy (Retd.) vs. Union of India
- Privacy as intrinsic part of Article 21
- Right to Life and Personal Liberty
- Evolving understanding in technology age

**Conclusion:**
Supreme Court's evolution reflects adaptive response to Indian democracy's needs; from safeguarding Fundamental Rights to dynamic constitutional interpretation and PIL access expansion; played indispensable role in shaping constitutional and democratic landscape; journey from legal interpretation body to active social justice participant underscores dynamic jurisdiction nature and critical governance role.

**Body:**

*Balance of Powers Disruption:*
- Key criticism: Disrupts delicate constitutional balance

- System of checks and balances
- Montesquieu and James Madison: Importance emphasized

*Proponents' Defense:*

- Role in social justice, environmental protection

Doctrine undoubtedly enhanced judicial review power; positions Court as pivotal check on legislative powers; through insights of Austin, Dhavan, and Baxi, evident doctrine played crucial role preserving Constitution's essence; ensuring democracy, secularism, and rule of law remain inviolable.
